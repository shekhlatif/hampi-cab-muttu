Lotus Mahal is one of the most beautiful and unique architectural structures in Hampi. Located inside the Zenana Enclosure, this historic palace is famous for its elegant design that resembles the shape of a blooming lotus flower. The monument represents a perfect blend of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles from the Vijayanagara period.

Built during the reign of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 15th century, Lotus Mahal served as a royal pavilion for the women of the royal family. The structure was likely used as a recreational space or meeting hall where the queens and princesses could relax and spend their leisure time.

The Vittala Temple complex is best known for the iconic Stone Chariot, which has become a symbol of Hampi and appears on the Indian ₹50 currency note.

Interesting Fact

Lotus Mahal is known for its unique cooling system. The palace was designed with multiple windows and corridors that allowed air to circulate, helping keep the structure cool even during hot summer days.

The palace stands on a raised platform and features beautifully carved arches, symmetrical towers, and delicate ornamental details. Its graceful architecture makes Lotus Mahal one of the most photographed monuments in Hampi.

Today, Lotus Mahal is one of the major attractions for tourists visiting Hampi. Located close to the famous Elephant Stables, the monument provides visitors with a glimpse into the luxurious lifestyle and advanced architectural knowledge of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Visitors exploring the Zenana Enclosure can also see nearby attractions such as Elephant Stables, watchtowers, and other historic structures that once formed part of the royal complex.
