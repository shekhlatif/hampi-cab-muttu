Vittala Temple - Hampi

Vittala Temple is one of the most magnificent monuments in Hampi.

Vittala Temple is one of the most magnificent monuments in Hampi and a masterpiece of the Vijayanagara Empire's architecture. Located near the banks of the Tungabhadra River, the temple is dedicated to Lord Vittala, a form of Lord Vishnu. It is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful and architecturally advanced temples in India.

The Vittala Temple complex is best known for the iconic Stone Chariot, which has become a symbol of Hampi and appears on the Indian ₹50 currency note.

Famous Attraction

The Vittala Temple complex is best known for the iconic Stone Chariot, which has become a symbol of Hampi and appears on the Indian ₹50 currency note.

Another extraordinary feature of the temple is its legendary musical pillars. These pillars were designed in such a way that when gently tapped they produce musical sounds. This remarkable engineering demonstrates the advanced knowledge and artistic excellence of the Vijayanagara craftsmen.

Today, the Vittala Temple is part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Hampi and attracts thousands of visitors every year. Tourists visiting Hampi consider this temple one of the most important landmarks because of its historical importance, architectural beauty, and cultural significance.

Exploring the Vittala Temple complex allows visitors to witness the grandeur of the Vijayanagara Empire and experience one of the most remarkable historical sites in India.
