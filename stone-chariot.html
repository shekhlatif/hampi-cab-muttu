Stone Chariot – Hampi

One of the most iconic monuments of the Vijayanagara Empire

The Stone Chariot in Hampi is one of the most iconic monuments in India and a symbol of the rich architectural heritage of the Vijayanagara Empire. Located inside the famous Vittala Temple complex, the chariot is designed in the shape of a traditional temple chariot and is carved entirely from granite.

The monument is dedicated to Garuda, the divine vehicle of Lord Vishnu. The wheels of the chariot were originally designed to rotate, showcasing the advanced engineering skills of ancient craftsmen during the Vijayanagara period.

Did You Know?

The Stone Chariot is part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Hampi and is considered one of the finest examples of ancient Indian stone architecture.

The detailed carvings and perfectly symmetrical structure make the Stone Chariot one of the most photographed landmarks in Hampi. Every year thousands of tourists visit this monument to admire its beauty and learn about the history of the Vijayanagara Empire.

For travelers exploring Hampi, the Stone Chariot is a must-visit attraction. Its historical significance, artistic craftsmanship, and cultural importance make it one of the highlights of any Hampi travel itinerary.
